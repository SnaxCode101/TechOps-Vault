1. Task that should be automated
One thing I do constantly is re-checking and updating patient status across multiple places tracking labs, imaging, and disposition plans. I’m bouncing between Epic, tracking boards, and sometimes handwritten notes just to keep a mental timeline.
For example, I’ll write down pending labs or CT results on paper or a scrap report sheet because it’s faster than digging back through the chart repeatedly. Then I still end up refreshing Epic or messaging providers for updates.
It feels like there should be an automated “patient timeline/status tracker” that consolidates:
•	Pending results
•	Consult status
•	Dispo plan
Right now it’s too manual and repetitive.
2. Time I had to track something down
This happens every shift. A typical example this week:
I had a patient waiting on CT results that were taking longer than expected. I had to:
•	Check Epic → saw “in progress”
•	Call CT → no answer
•	Physically walk over to CT
•	Ask the tech if it was done
•	Then track down the provider to review it
All while managing other patients.
What I needed was a clear answer: Is the scan done? Has it been read? What’s the delay?
Instead, I had to piece that together across systems and people.
3. Most annoying part of hand-off
Shift-to-shift report is honestly one of the most frustrating parts when the system doesn’t support it well.
The issue isn’t giving report it’s:
•	Information being buried in long notes
•	Inconsistent documentation styles
•	Important updates not clearly flagged (like “waiting for admit bed” vs “possible discharge”)
So I end up doing my own mini-chart review anyway after getting report just to verify everything.
Also, when handing it off to another department (like admitting or transport), there’s often no clear confirmation loop. You place the request, but you don’t always know:
•	Did they see it?
•	Are they coming?
•	Is there a delay?
4. “Magic button” on screen
If I could have one button, it would be:
“Patient Status Update / Coordination Button”
It would:
•	Ping all relevant departments (lab, imaging, transport, provider)
•	Show real-time status updates in one view
•	Auto-notify me if something is delayed beyond a threshold
OR even more simply:
“Where is everything for this patient?” button
→ One click summary of: labs, imaging, consults, dispo barriers
That would save so much time and mental load.
5. Workaround I use (not official)
I definitely keep my own handwritten “brain sheet” during shifts.
It includes:
•	Room numbers
•	Key labs pending
•	Pain meds timing
•	Tasks I need to circle back to
It’s faster than navigating Epic for quick checks, especially when things get busy.
Also, informal communication shortcuts:
•	Grabbing providers or techs in person instead of waiting on messages/orders
•	Asking verbally for updates before they’re officially documented
It’s not the “official” workflow, but it’s the only way to keep things moving in real time.




**“From my perspective as an ER nurse at Lowell General Hospital, the recent Stryker cyberattack didn’t directly disrupt my day-to-day tools like Epic or medication systems. I was still able to chart, give meds, and manage patients without system downtime.**

Where it did matter was indirectly. We rely heavily on vendors like Stryker for equipment and supplies, so when something like that happens, it can have an impact on surgical scheduling, supply availability, and ultimately patient flow. In the ED, that shows up as longer boarding times and delays in getting patients to the OR or inpatient beds. So even if our internal systems are stable, we’re still vulnerable to disruptions outside the hospital.

**In terms of my day-to-day workflow, I primarily use Epic, the tracking board, secure messaging, and medication dispensing systems like Pyxis.**

These tools are essential and generally reliable, but there are definitely areas where things could be improved.

One of the biggest challenges is fragmentation of information. I have to click through multiple tabs to piece together a full picture of what’s going on with patient labs, imaging, consults, and dispo plans are all in different places. There’s no single, clean “status view,” so I end up double-checking things or even writing them down to keep track.

Another major issue is lack of real-time visibility. When I order something like a CT scan, transport, or a consult, I don’t have a clear sense of where that request stands. I often have to call or physically track people down, which interrupts workflow and takes time away from patient care.

Handoffs are also a pain point**.** Even with report, information can be inconsistent or buried in notes, so I still feel like I need to re-verify everything myself to be safe.

If I could improve anything, it would be creating a more unified and transparent system. Ideally, I’d have a single dashboard that shows:

- what’s been ordered
- what’s pending
- What’s delayed
- and what’s blocking disposition

I’d also love real-time task tracking almost like how you can track a ride or delivery so I know when transport, imaging, or consults are actually in progress.
